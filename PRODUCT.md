# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Hackathon judges, Blackbird/Flynet partners, and diners evaluating how Flynet can turn restaurant discovery into an actionable route.

## Product Purpose

Passport Quest builds customized restaurant routes from Flynet Discovery data, taste signals, and location context. Success means a user can pick a taste lane, generate a plausible multi-stop route, inspect it on a real map, and open directions without the interface feeling like a prototype.

## Brand Personality

Practical, urban, quietly premium.

## Anti-references

Avoid game-show roulette, generic hackathon dashboard cards, marketing-section filler, fake illustrated maps, and visual noise that competes with restaurant data.

## Design Principles

- Treat the route as the product, map and itinerary first.
- Use Blackbird tokens and branded components instead of one-off styling.
- Keep personalization visible, but make quests configurable instead of hardcoded.
- Make every action useful: generate, inspect, reserve, and open directions.
- Favor dense, trustworthy product UI over decorative spectacle.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, readable mobile typography, and reduced-motion-safe state changes.
